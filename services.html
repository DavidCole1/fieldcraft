Beyond the basics

Things I can build in

Not every business needs these — but when they fit, they're the features that take a site from a brochure to a tool that saves you hours.

Instant quote calculators

A short set of questions that gives a customer a real price range and emails it to them — so the jobs that reach your phone are the ones worth taking. Built one of these for Lett's Property Care.

Before & after galleries

Structured photo comparisons that show the actual difference you make. For trades where the result is visual, nothing else sells as hard.

Hiring & application forms

If you're growing a crew, an application form on your own site beats reposting a job listing every few weeks.

Service-area pages

A page per town you serve, so you turn up in searches across your whole coverage area rather than just your home city.

Review integration

Your real Google, Yelp, or Nextdoor reviews placed where they'll actually change a decision — not buried on a testimonials page nobody visits.

Booking & scheduling links

If you already use a scheduling tool, I'll wire it in properly so customers can book without a phone call.

Honest limits

What I don't do

I'd rather tell you now than take a deposit for something I'm not the right person to build.

  • Full e-commerce stores. If you're selling hundreds of products with inventory and shipping, you want Shopify and a specialist.
  • Custom web apps. Customer portals, dashboards, anything with logins and databases is a different discipline.
  • Logo and brand identity from scratch. I'll work beautifully with the brand you have. If you need one designed, I'll point you to someone who does that properly.
  • Paid advertising management. I build the site the ads point at. Running the ad spend is somebody else's job.
  • Guaranteed Google rankings. Nobody can honestly promise that. I do the setup correctly and let the work compound.
