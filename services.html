Beyond the basics

Things I can build in

Not every business needs these. When they fit, they turn a site from a brochure into something that saves you hours every week.

Instant quote calculators

A few questions that give the customer a real price range and email it to them, so the jobs reaching your phone are the ones worth taking. I built one of these for Lett's Property Care.

Before & after galleries

Side-by-side photos showing the difference you made. In trades where the result is visual, nothing sells harder.

Hiring & application forms

If you're growing a crew, an application form on your own site beats reposting a listing every few weeks.

Service-area pages

A page for each town you serve, so you turn up across your whole coverage area instead of just your home city.

Review integration

Your real Google, Yelp, or Nextdoor reviews put where they'll change someone's mind, instead of buried on a testimonials page nobody opens.

Booking & scheduling links

Already use a scheduling tool? I'll wire it in so customers can book without calling.

Honest limits

What I don't do

Better you hear this now than after I've taken a deposit for something I'm not the right person to build.

  • Full e-commerce stores. Selling hundreds of products with inventory and shipping? You want Shopify and a specialist.
  • Custom web apps. Customer portals, dashboards, anything with logins and a database. Different discipline.
  • Logo and brand identity from scratch. I'll work with the brand you have. If you need one made, I'll point you to someone who does that for a living.
  • Paid advertising management. I build the site the ads point at. Someone else should run the spend.
  • Guaranteed Google rankings. Nobody can honestly promise that. I do the setup right and let it compound.
