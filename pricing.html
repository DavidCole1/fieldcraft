The Care Plan

What you get every month

This is the part people ask about most, so here's what happens and what doesn't.

Included

  • Full site check, every page loaded and verified
  • Broken link and load speed monitoring
  • Up to 3 content updates a month (hours, prices, photos, text)
  • One Google Business Profile post
  • SSL and security verification
  • A plain-English monthly report
  • Direct access to me for questions

Not included

  • New pages or major redesigns, quoted separately
  • New functionality like booking systems or calculators
  • Photography or logo design
  • Paid ad management
  • Domain and hosting, billed at cost, usually $15 to $25 a year

A "small update" is anything under about 30 minutes. Ask for something bigger and I'll tell you the cost first, instead of doing it quietly and putting it on an invoice later.

Questions

About money, specifically

Do I have to pay before I see anything?
No. You get a free one-page sample in your own name and branding first. You only pay if you want the full site built. If you don't like it, you owe nothing.
Is the Care Plan required?
No. You can take the site and manage it yourself, and you own it either way. It's cancellable with 30 days' notice. Take it if you'd rather not think about upkeep.
What if I need more revisions than my tier includes?
Extra rounds are $150 each and always agreed in writing first. I ask for feedback in one message rather than a trickle of changes, which is meant to keep most builds inside what's included.
Are there hidden costs?
Two, both small, both billed at cost with no markup. A domain runs about $15 to $25 a year if you don't own one, and hosting is often free at this size. Everything else is in the number you're quoted.
What if I want to stop halfway through?
The deposit covers work already done, so it isn't refundable. You owe nothing beyond that and you keep everything built so far. No lock-in and no cancellation fee.
Why is this cheaper than an agency?
There's no office, no account managers, and no sales team. I use modern tools, AI included, for first drafts of code and copy, then review every line myself. The savings come out of overhead, not out of a discount I'll claw back later.
Can I pay in installments?
Standard is 50% up front and 50% at launch. If a build is big enough that a different schedule suits your cash flow better, just ask. I'd rather work around timing than lose a project over it.
