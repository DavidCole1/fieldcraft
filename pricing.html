The Care Plan

What you get every month

This is the part people question most, so here's exactly what happens — and what doesn't.

Included

  • Full site check — every page loaded and verified
  • Broken link and load speed monitoring
  • Up to 3 content updates a month (hours, prices, photos, text)
  • One Google Business Profile post
  • SSL and security verification
  • A plain-English monthly report
  • Direct access to me for questions

Not included

  • New pages or major redesigns — quoted separately
  • New functionality like booking systems or calculators
  • Photography or logo design
  • Paid ad management
  • Domain and hosting fees — billed at cost, usually $15–$25/year

A "small update" means something that takes under 30 minutes. If you ask for something bigger, I'll tell you up front what it costs rather than quietly doing it and invoicing you later.

Questions

About money, specifically

Do I have to pay before I see anything?
No. You get a free one-page sample in your own name and branding first. You only pay if you want the full site built. If you don't like it, you owe nothing.
Is the Care Plan required?
No. You can take the site and manage it yourself — you own it either way. It's optional, cancellable with 30 days' notice — take it if you'd rather not think about upkeep.
What if I need more revisions than my tier includes?
Additional rounds are $150 each, always agreed in writing before I start. I ask for feedback in one consolidated message rather than trickling changes back and forth, which is designed to keep builds inside the included rounds.
Are there hidden costs?
Two, both small and both billed at cost with no markup: a domain name (roughly $15–$25/year if you don't already own one) and hosting (often free at this scale). Everything else is in the price you're quoted.
What if I want to stop halfway through?
The deposit covers work already done and isn't refundable, but you owe nothing further and you receive everything built to that point. No lock-in, no cancellation penalty.
Why is this cheaper than an agency?
No office, no account managers, no sales team. I use modern tooling — including AI for first drafts of code and copy — and review every line myself. The savings are structural overhead, not a discount I'll claw back later.
Can I pay in installments?
The standard split is 50% up front and 50% at launch. If a build is large enough that a different schedule makes sense for your cash flow, ask — I'd rather structure it so it works than lose the project over timing.
